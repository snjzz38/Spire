Academic Planning

Build a strong academic foundation that will impress college admissions officers and prepare you for university success.

Course Selection Strategy

Choosing the right courses is crucial for demonstrating academic rigor and preparing for your intended major. Here's how to build a competitive course schedule:

Core Academic Requirements

Most competitive universities expect to see a strong foundation in core subjects:

  • English: 4 years of literature, composition, and critical analysis
  • Mathematics: Through calculus if possible (Algebra I & II, Geometry, Pre-Calculus, Calculus)
  • Science: 3-4 years including Biology, Chemistry, and Physics
  • Social Studies: 3-4 years including World History, US History, and Government
  • Foreign Language: 2-4 years of the same language

Advanced Placement (AP) Courses

AP courses demonstrate your ability to handle college-level work. Consider these strategies:

  1. Start gradually: Take 1-2 AP courses in sophomore year
  2. Build momentum: Increase to 3-4 AP courses in junior year
  3. Focus on strengths: Choose APs related to your intended major
  4. Balance your load: Don't sacrifice grades for quantity

GPA Improvement Strategies

Your GPA is one of the most important factors in college admissions. Here are proven methods to boost your academic performance:

  • Create a consistent study schedule and stick to it
  • Form study groups with motivated classmates
  • Attend teacher office hours for extra help
  • Use active learning techniques like flashcards and practice tests
  • Seek tutoring for challenging subjects early, not when you're already struggling

Standardized Test Preparation

Strong SAT or ACT scores can significantly boost your college applications. Here's a comprehensive approach to test prep:

Choosing Between SAT and ACT

Take practice tests for both to see which format suits you better:

  • SAT: More time per question, focuses on reasoning and problem-solving
  • ACT: Faster pace, includes science section, more straightforward questions

Test Prep Timeline

  1. Junior Year Fall: Take diagnostic practice tests
  2. Junior Year Winter: Begin structured prep (3-6 months)
  3. Junior Year Spring: Take the actual test for the first time
  4. Senior Year Fall: Retake if needed for score improvement

Effective Study Methods

Maximize your prep time with these proven strategies:

  • Focus on your weakest areas first
  • Take full-length practice tests under timed conditions
  • Review every wrong answer to understand your mistakes
  • Learn test-taking strategies specific to each section
  • Consider a prep course or tutor if self-study isn't working

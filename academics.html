Academics

Build a strong academic foundation for your university applications with strategic course selection and academic planning.

Course Selection Strategy

Your course selection plays a crucial role in university admissions. Admissions officers look for students who challenge themselves academically while maintaining strong performance.

Key Steps for Academic Success

  1. Choose Rigorous Courses: Take AP, IB, or honors classes in core subjects like English, Math, Science, and Social Studies.
  2. Maintain Balance: Don't overload yourself. It's better to excel in fewer challenging courses than to struggle in too many.
  3. Show Progression: Demonstrate growth by taking increasingly challenging courses each year.
  4. Focus on Your Interests: Take advanced courses in subjects related to your intended major or career interests.
  5. Meet Requirements: Ensure you fulfill all graduation requirements and university prerequisites.

Standardized Testing

Most universities require standardized test scores. Plan your testing schedule early and consider taking tests multiple times to improve your scores. Check with your target universities to see if they require the SAT or ACT, and whether subject tests are needed.

GPA and Class Rank

Your Grade Point Average (GPA) and class rank are important factors in university admissions. Here's what you need to know:

Understanding GPA Systems

  • Unweighted GPA: Calculated on a 4.0 scale without considering course difficulty
  • Weighted GPA: Gives extra points for honors, AP, and IB courses
  • College GPA: Some universities recalculate your GPA using their own system

Focus on taking challenging courses and maintaining strong grades. Universities prefer to see a strong performance in rigorous courses rather than perfect grades in easy classes.

Opportunities in High School

High school is about more than just classes and homework. It's a time to discover what you're passionate about and build skills for your future.

Why You Should Bother

Looking for extracurriculars, internships, and scholarships isn't just about padding your resume. These opportunities help you:

  • Discover your passions: Figure out what you genuinely enjoy doing.
  • Gain real-world skills: Learn teamwork, problem-solving, and communication outside of a classroom.
  • Get recognized: Stand out to colleges and for scholarships.

How to Find What You're Looking For

Your passions or interests should be the driving factor in finding your opportunities. Almost, every opportunity should line up with the type of profile, spike, or mission you are trying to convey to colleges. Finding opportunities is a mix of online and local searching. A few key places to start:

  • Online Databases: There are many websites and databases dedicated to compiling opportunities. These are a great starting point for finding national and international competitions and programs.
  • Local Communities: Don't forget to look in your own backyard! Local businesses, non-profits, and universities often have unique internships and scholarships that aren't widely advertised.
  • School Resources: Your high school counselor is a fantastic resource. They often have information about local internships and school-specific programs.

Ready to Start Your Search?

To save you some time, here are some great resources compiled by other students and organizations. Use them to kickstart your search!

Remember, the best opportunities are often the ones you seek out yourself. Happy hunting!

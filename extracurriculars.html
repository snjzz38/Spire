Tier 3: Active Participation and Minor Leadership

This tier represents meaningful and consistent involvement in your activities. It shows that you are a reliable and engaged member of your community, which is a strong indicator to admissions officers of how you might participate in campus life.

  • Consistent Commitment: You are a dedicated member of a sports team, club, or volunteer organization, participating regularly over a significant period.
  • Minor Leadership Roles: You have taken on some responsibility, such as being a club treasurer, a section leader in the band, or the captain of the junior varsity team.
  • Substantive Contribution: You actively contribute to the group's goals, even without a formal title. For example, you might be the go-to person for organizing events or managing a specific project.

Examples: Being a dedicated member of the debate team for three years, consistently volunteering at a local animal shelter every weekend, or holding the position of secretary in the student council.

Tier 4: General Membership and Participation

These activities are the most common and represent the starting point of engagement. While they don't carry the same weight as higher-tiered activities, they are still valuable for showing that you are willing to explore different interests and be part of a group.

  • General Membership: You are a member of several clubs but don't hold a specific leadership position or have major responsibilities.
  • Sporadic or Short-Term Involvement: This could include volunteering for a one-time event or joining a club for a single semester.
  • Exploring Interests: These activities often show a breadth of interests and a willingness to try new things.

Examples: Being a general member of the French club, participating in a school-wide charity drive for a week, or attending meetings for the environmental club.

Key Takeaway: Show Growth and Impact

The goal is to demonstrate a positive trajectory. An ideal application might show a journey where you started with Tier 4 activities (exploring interests) and progressed to Tier 3 activities (deeper commitment and minor leadership) in the areas you became most passionate about. This creates a compelling story of your personal and intellectual development.
